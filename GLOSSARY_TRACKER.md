| cross-validation | 5.2 Train/Val/Test & Cross-Validation | A technique that splits the training data into k folds, rotating which fold is held out as validation each round, training k times and averaging the results — a more robust estimate than a single validation split, especially useful with limited data. |

| L1/L2 regularization | 5.4 Regularization | Two common penalty shapes: L1 penalizes the sum of the absolute values of the weights and tends to push unhelpful weights all the way to zero. | ReLU (Rectified Linear Unit) | 6.3 Activation Functions | An activation function that outputs 0 for any negative input, and passes positive input straight through unchanged: ReLU(x) = max(0, x). Cheap to compute and the most widely used activation function in modern deep networks. |
| backpropagation | 6.4 Backpropagation | The algorithm that computes the loss's gradient with respect to every weight in a neural network, by applying the chain rule repeatedly, working backward from the output layer to the input layer one layer at a time. |
| stochastic gradient descent (SGD) | 6.5 Optimizers (SGD, Adam) | Gradient descent computed on a small random subset of the training data at each step instead of the entire dataset at once — much cheaper per step, and the resulting noise can even help escape shallow traps, at the cost of a noisier path. |
| momentum | 6.5 Optimizers (SGD, Adam) | An optimizer that accumulates a running velocity from past gradients instead of reacting only to the current one — like a ball rolling downhill that keeps some of its speed, smoothing out zigzags and powering through small flat patches. |
| Adam | 6.5 Optimizers (SGD, Adam) | An optimizer combining momentum with a per-parameter adaptive step size, based on how large that parameter's recent gradients have typically been. The most common default optimizer for training neural networks in practice. |
| batch | 6.6 The Training Toolbox | The chunk of training examples used to compute one gradient and take one optimizer step. Also called a mini-batch. Small compared to the full dataset — a middle ground between using one example at a time and the entire dataset at once. |
| epoch | 6.6 The Training Toolbox | One complete pass through the entire training dataset. A model is typically trained for many epochs, seeing every training example repeatedly. |
| learning rate schedule | 6.6 The Training Toolbox | A plan for changing the learning rate over the course of training, instead of holding it fixed — typically starting higher, for fast early progress, and decreasing over time for careful, stable convergence. |
| vanishing gradient | 6.7 Vanishing Gradients & Initialization | A gradient that shrinks toward zero as it's propagated backward through many layers, because each layer's local derivative is a fraction less than 1, and those fractions multiply together. | weight initialization | 6.7 Vanishing Gradients & Initialization | The choice of starting values for a network's weights before training begins. | batch normalization (BatchNorm) | 6.8 Dropout & BatchNorm | A technique that renormalizes a layer's activations back to a stable, consistent scale after every layer, throughout training — not just at initialization.
